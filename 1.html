1.1 The French Revolution

1.1.1 The Storming of the Bastille

  • 14 July 1789: The city of Paris was in a state of alarm.
  • Royal Troops: The king had commanded troops to move into the city, causing panic.
  • Rumours: Spread that the king would order the army to open fire on citizens.
  • People's Militia: Around 7,000 men and women gathered in front of the town hall and decided to form a militia.
  • Search for Arms: They broke into government buildings to find arms.

1.1.3 The Beginning of Revolutionary Events

  • Rioting: Continued both in Paris and the countryside, largely in protest of high bread prices.
  • Historical Perspective: Historians view this period as the start of a series of events leading to the king's execution.
  • Public Uncertainty: At the time, most people did not foresee the revolution leading to such drastic outcomes.
