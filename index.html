Chapter 1: Number Systems - Introduction

Types of Numbers

1. Natural Numbers (N):

Definition: The set of positive integers starting from 1.

Examples: 1, 2, 3, 4, ...

2. Whole Numbers (W):

Definition: The set of natural numbers including 0.

Examples: 0, 1, 2, 3, 4, ...

3. Integers (Z):

Definition: The set of whole numbers and their negatives.

Examples: ..., -3, -2, -1, 0, 1, 2, 3, ...

4. Rational Numbers (Q):

Definition: Numbers that can be expressed in the form \( \frac{p}{q} \), where \( p \) and \( q \) are integers and \( q \neq 0 \).

Examples: \( \frac{1}{2}, \frac{-3}{4}, 5 \) (since \( 5 = \frac{5}{1} \))

5. Irrational Numbers:

Definition: Numbers that cannot be expressed in the form \( \frac{p}{q} \), where \( p \) and \( q \) are integers and \( q \neq 0 \). These numbers have non-terminating, non-repeating decimal expansions.

Examples: \( \sqrt{2}, \pi \)

6. Real Numbers (R):

Definition: The set of all rational and irrational numbers.

Examples: 1, -3, \( \frac{4}{5} \), \( \sqrt{3} \), \( \pi \)

Important Questions

Question 1: Classify the following numbers into natural numbers, whole numbers, integers, rational numbers, and irrational numbers: -2, 0, \( \frac{7}{3} \), 5, \( \sqrt{5} \), \( \pi \)

Answer 1:

  • Natural Numbers: 5
  • Whole Numbers: 0, 5
  • Integers: -2, 0, 5
  • Rational Numbers: 0, \( \frac{7}{3} \), 5
  • Irrational Numbers: \( \sqrt{5} \), \( \pi \)

Question 2: Determine if the following statements are true or false:

(a) Every whole number is a natural number.

(b) Every integer is a rational number.

(c) Every rational number is an integer.

(d) Every irrational number is a real number.

Answer 2:

  • (a) False (since 0 is a whole number but not a natural number)
  • (b) True (every integer can be written as \( \frac{p}{1} \))
  • (c) False (rational numbers include fractions that are not integers)
  • (d) True (irrational numbers are part of real numbers)

Concept Understanding

The introduction to the number system forms the base for understanding different properties and operations of numbers in subsequent chapters. Mastery of these classifications will aid in solving complex problems involving different types of numbers.
