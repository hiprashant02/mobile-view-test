Number Systems

1.1 Introduction

In mathematics, the number system is a way to represent numbers. Numbers are an integral part of our daily lives and mathematics. The number system helps us classify and understand different types of numbers. Let's explore the various types of numbers in the number system:

Natural Numbers (N)

Natural numbers are the basic counting numbers. They start from 1 and go on infinitely.

  • Examples: 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, ...

Whole Numbers (W)

Whole numbers include all natural numbers along with zero.

  • Examples: 0, 1, 2, 3, 4, ...

Integers (Z)

Integers include all whole numbers and their negative counterparts. Integers extend infinitely in both the positive and negative directions.

  • Examples: ..., -3, -2, -1, 0, 1, 2, 3, ...

Rational Numbers (Q)

Rational numbers are numbers that can be expressed as a fraction p/q, where p and q are integers, and q ≠ 0. Rational numbers include integers, fractions, and terminating or repeating decimals.

  • Examples:
    • 1/2 = 0.5
    • 4/3 = 1.333...

Irrational Numbers

Irrational numbers cannot be expressed as a simple fraction p/q. They have non-terminating and non-repeating decimal expansions.

  • Examples:
    • √2 = 1.414213...
    • π = 3.141592...

Real Numbers (R)

Real numbers include all rational and irrational numbers. Every point on the number line represents a real number.

  • Examples: 2, -3, 0.75, √3, π

Classification of Numbers

  • Natural Numbers (N): {1, 2, 3, 4, 5, ...}
  • Whole Numbers (W): {0, 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, ...}
  • Integers (Z): {..., -3, -2, -1, 0, 1, 2, 3, ...}
  • Rational Numbers (Q): Numbers that can be written as p/q where p and q are integers and q ≠ 0
  • Irrational Numbers: Numbers that cannot be written as p/q
  • Real Numbers (R): All rational and irrational numbers

Important Points

  • Natural numbers are used for counting.
  • Whole numbers include zero along with natural numbers.
  • Integers expand whole numbers to include negative values.
  • Rational numbers are fractions or ratios of integers.
  • Irrational numbers cannot be expressed as fractions and have unique decimal expansions.
  • Real numbers encompass both rational and irrational numbers, covering the entire number line.

Understanding these classifications and their properties helps in recognizing different types of numbers and their applications in various mathematical contexts.
