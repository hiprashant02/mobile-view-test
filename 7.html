1.7 The Revolution and Everyday Life

1.7.1 Changes in Daily Life

  • The French Revolution brought about significant changes in the daily lives of the people.
  • The revolutionary government took steps to translate the ideals of liberty and equality into everyday practice.
  • One important step was the abolition of censorship, which allowed for freedom of speech and expression.

1.7.2 The Tricolor Flag

  • A new tricolor flag was adopted to replace the royal standard.
  • The tricolor flag became a symbol of the Republic and national unity.

1.7.3 New Forms of Address

  • The traditional forms of address such as ‘Monsieur’ (Sir) and ‘Madame’ (Madam) were replaced by the more egalitarian ‘Citizen’ (Citoyen) and ‘Citizens’ (Citoyenne).
  • This change was meant to emphasize the idea of equality among all citizens.

1.7.4 Clothing

  • Revolutionaries promoted the wearing of simple, practical clothes to reflect the ideals of simplicity and equality.
  • The wearing of knee breeches was abandoned, and people started wearing long trousers.
  • Liberty caps, also known as the Phrygian cap, became popular as a symbol of freedom.

1.7.5 The Revolutionary Calendar

  • The revolutionary government introduced a new calendar to break away from the past and the influence of the Church.
  • This calendar had 12 months, each consisting of 30 days, divided into three ten-day weeks called ‘decades’.
  • The names of the months were derived from nature, such as ‘Vendémiaire’ (vintage), ‘Brumaire’ (fog), and ‘Frimaire’ (frost).
  • However, this calendar was abolished by Napoleon in 1805.

1.7.6 The Metric System

  • The revolutionaries introduced the metric system to standardize weights and measures.
  • This system was based on units of ten and is still used today in many parts of the world.
