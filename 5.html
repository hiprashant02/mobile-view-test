1.5 Did Women Have a Revolution?

1.5.1 The Role of Women

  • From the beginning, women were active participants in the events which brought about the French Revolution.
  • They hoped that their involvement would pressurize the revolutionary government to introduce measures to improve their lives.
  • Most women of the third estate had to work for a living. They sold vegetables, fruits, flowers, worked as seamstresses, laundresses, or as domestic servants.
  • Working women also had to care for their families and were paid lower wages than men.

1.5.2 Political Clubs and the Demands of Women

  • To discuss and voice their interests, women started their own political clubs and newspapers.
  • About sixty women’s clubs came up in different French cities.
  • The most famous was the Society of Revolutionary and Republican Women.
  • They demanded the same political rights as men, such as the right to vote, to be elected to the Assembly, and to hold political office.

1.5.3 Laws for Women

  • During the Reign of Terror, the government issued laws ordering the closure of women’s clubs and banning their political activities.
  • Women were not granted the same political rights as men.
  • However, revolutionary governments introduced laws to improve the lives of women:
    • Schooling was made compulsory for all girls.
    • Fathers could no longer force their daughters into marriage against their will.
    • Marriage was made into a contract entered into freely and registered under civil law.
    • Divorce was made legal, and could be applied for by both women and men.

1.5.4 The Struggle Continues

  • Women’s struggle for equal political rights, however, continued.
  • It was finally in 1946 that women in France won the right to vote.
