1.4 France Abolishes Monarchy and Becomes a Republic

1.4.1 The National Assembly

  • 1791: The National Assembly completed the draft of the constitution.
  • The main objective was to limit the powers of the monarch and establish a constitutional monarchy.
  • The new constitution gave the power to make laws to the National Assembly, which was elected by a group of active citizens.
  • Active Citizens: Only men above 25 years of age who paid taxes equivalent to at least 3 days of a laborer's wage were considered active citizens and could vote.

1.4.2 The Reign of Terror

  • 1793-1794: A period known as the Reign of Terror under the rule of Maximilien Robespierre.
  • Robespierre followed a policy of severe control and punishment to defend the republic from its enemies.
  • Thousands of suspected enemies were arrested, tried, and executed by the guillotine.
  • Guillotine: A device used during the French Revolution for beheading people, seen as a symbol of the Reign of Terror.
  • Robespierre's policies included rationing of bread, use of revolutionary tribunals, and a system of revolutionary laws.
  • Robespierre was eventually arrested and executed in July 1794, ending the Reign of Terror.

1.4.3 The Directory Rules France

  • After the fall of Robespierre, a new constitution was introduced in 1795.
  • It provided for two elected legislative councils which appointed an executive body known as the Directory.
  • The Directory was made up of five members and was meant to prevent the concentration of power in one individual.
  • However, the Directory faced constant conflicts with the legislative councils and was unable to solve the economic crises.
  • This political instability paved the way for the rise of Napoleon Bonaparte.
