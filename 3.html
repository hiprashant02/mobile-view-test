1.3 The Outbreak of the Revolution

1.3.1 The Estates-General

  • 5 May 1789: Louis XVI called a meeting of the Estates-General to pass proposals for new taxes.
  • Estates-General: An assembly of representatives from all three estates.
  • Voting System: Traditionally, each estate had one vote. The Third Estate demanded that each representative have one vote, but the king rejected this proposal.
  • This rejection led to the formation of the National Assembly by the Third Estate representatives.

1.3.2 The Tennis Court Oath

  • 20 June 1789: The National Assembly was locked out of their meeting hall.
  • They moved to a nearby indoor tennis court and took the Tennis Court Oath: Vowing not to disperse until a new constitution was created.
  • This event marked the first step towards the creation of a constitution in France.

1.3.3 The Fall of the Bastille

  • 14 July 1789: A significant event where people stormed the Bastille, a fortress-prison in Paris.
  • The Bastille was a symbol of the king's despotic rule, and its fall marked the beginning of the French Revolution.
  • Aftermath: The commander of the Bastille was killed, prisoners were released, and the fortress was demolished.
  • The fall of the Bastille ignited widespread rebellion across France, leading to the formation of a people's militia.
